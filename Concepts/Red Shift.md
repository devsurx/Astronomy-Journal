When an object emitting light moves **away** from you, the light waves stretch out — wavelength increases, which shifts the light toward the **red** end of the spectrum (red light has longer wavelengths than blue/violet). Hence "redshift."

# In context of Black Holes:
**Gravitational redshift** — light climbing out of a strong gravitational field loses energy and gets redshifted, purely from gravity's effect, light escaping from near a black hole gets significantly redshifted, and light can't escape at all past the event horizon, which is sort of the "infinite redshift" limit.
