**In General Relativity**:
- Not a point in space, but ==a point in time==
- When an object approaches the [[Event Horizon]] ==the singularity lies in your future==
- One can say that space and time become so distorted that they get swapped such that the singularity is now a point in your future
- This may be visualized by a Penrose diagram

![[Penrose Diagram.png]]

This whole four-region picture only applies to an idealized, ==**eternal, non-rotating black hole** with nothing else in the universe — a pure math solution (Schwarzschild spacetime).== Real black holes that form from a collapsing star don't have the white hole or parallel universe regions at all — those only show up if you eternally extend the math, which real astrophysical black holes don't. It's a great tool for understanding causal structure but not literally what's out there.
