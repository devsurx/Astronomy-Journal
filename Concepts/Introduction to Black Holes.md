
**Definition**: 
- Einstein's theory of relativity
	- Extreme distortions in space and time, with a gravitational pull so strong that once an object crosses the [[Event Horizon]] it cannot escape no matter the speed

**Formation**:
- A star nearing its end collapses into itself and when the stars radius equals the [[Schwarzschild Radius]] its compressed enough to become a black hole
- [[Chandrasekhar Limit]] (For stars collapsing into a neutron star)
- [[The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) limit]] (For neutron stars collapsing into a black hole)
- Stars that are low to medium solar mass collapse into a neutron star, ==but when a star with a solar mass of 20-25 runs out of fuel it blows past the neutron star phase and collapses into a blackhole in a fraction of a second==
- When a star like this collapses, gravity wins completely and ==crushes the star down into a singularity creating a black hole==
- [[Singularity]]

**River Model (To Visualize)**:
The river of space flows in such a way into the black hole that even if a fish (object) swims outwards at the speed of light it will still inevitably get sucked inside

Supermassive blackholes like the one at the center of our galaxy [[Sagittarius A-star]] or the [[M87-star]] were most likely formed by direct collision of smaller black holes after [[The Big Bang]]

**Falling into a black hole**:
- Smaller black holes: The [[Tidal Force]] is a lot more imminent and stronger so you undergo [[Spaghettification]] almost immediately as you cross the [[Event Horizon]] 

- Larger Black Holes: The [[Tidal Force]] is not as strong as in a smaller BH due to these being a lot larger in size, the [[Singularity]] still inevitably lies in your future but you may cross the [[Event Horizon]] and not feel anything for some time but you will still undergo [[Spaghettification]] sooner or later
