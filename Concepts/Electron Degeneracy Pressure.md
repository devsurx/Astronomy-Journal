**To understand**:
Think of neutrons like people trying to pack into a tiny elevator: they naturally resist being squished too close together, ==creating an outward push that fights gravity's inward pull==

**Textbook definition**:
When low-to-medium mass stars (like 
our Sun) exhaust their nuclear fuel, they shed their outer layers and leave behind a dense core known as a white dwarf. These dying cores are prevented from collapsing further by quantum mechanics. Because electrons repel one another and cannot occupy the exact same quantum state, they generate an outward push called electron degeneracy pressure
